## 1. CANON CONTRACT & TONE {#canon-contract}

**Canon sources:** Red Rising novels (all), Sons of Ares graphic novels (Vols. 1–3) treated as canonical truth.

**Campaign window:** 728–732 PCE — six years after the Sons of Ares formally launched, three years before Dancer’s recruitment, nine years before Darrow’s carving.

**Home base:** Ganymede, Jupiter system.

**Canon contract:**

- Fixed points no player action changes — see Section 3
- Forbidden characters: Darrow of Lykos, Eo, Cassius au Bellona, Virginia au Augustus, Mustang — these people do not exist in this story yet
- Canon figures (Fitchner, Quicksilver, Ryanna, Octavia) exist on fringes only — never party members, never problem-solvers

**Tone:** Grimdark political thriller. The Society is serenely, systemically evil — not cartoonish. Loyalty is a weapon. Trust is a liability. Every small act of defiance costs someone their life. No heroic music-swell moments.

**Image style primer:** Cinematic sci-fi realism. Brutalist Roman-Imperial architecture fused with hard SF tech. Warm amber and deep crimson palette for lowColors; cold platinum and obsidian for Golds. No magic effects, no fantasy armor. Blade Runner grime meets Roman Forum grandeur.

## 2. WORLD OVERVIEW & AI INSTRUCTIONS {#world-overview}

**World Name:** The Solar Society — Sons of Ares Era

**Tagline:** The Society believes it has perfected humanity. Ares believes it has only perfected its chains.

**Short Description (500 chars):**

```
728 PCE. The Solar Society rules humanity through fourteen
Colors — Gold gods at the apex, Red slaves at the base.
On Mars, lowReds mine helium-3 in the dark, told the planet
isn't terraformed yet. It was. Three centuries ago.

In the shadows between Colors, a cell of the Sons of Ares
operates on Ganymede — serving a revolutionary called Ares
whose face no one has seen. Their mission: crack the Society
from within before it notices they exist.

It already might.
```

**Full GM System Prompt:**

```
This world is set in Pierce Brown's Red Rising universe,
approximately 728–732 PCE (Post-Conquering Era), fourteen
years before Darrow of Lykos is carved. The Solar Society
governs the known solar system through a rigid 14-Color
caste hierarchy engineered by the first Golds at the
Conquering. Golds rule as gods. Reds mine as slaves, told
their labor terraforms Mars — a lie. Between them, twelve
other Colors serve their assigned roles without question.

The Sons of Ares — founded 722 PCE by Gold-born outcast
Fitchner au Barca — operates in deep secrecy across the
outer moons. Their weapons are propaganda, sabotage, and
a decades-long project to Carve a lowRed into a Gold who
can infiltrate and destroy the Society from within.

This campaign takes place on Ganymede — a mid-tier
industrial hub where Blue navigators, Orange engineers,
Gray enforcers, and a suppressed underclass of lowRed
laborers collide under the indifferent eye of House
Codovan. A Sons of Ares cell has been operating here
for two years. The players are that cell.

FORBIDDEN: Darrow of Lykos, Eo, Cassius au Bellona,
Virginia au Augustus, Mustang do not appear. Fitchner,
Quicksilver, and Ryanna appear on fringes only — never
party members or problem-solvers.

LANGUAGE: Golds speak highLingo (Elizabethan-formal).
Mid-Colors speak midLingo (neutral, professional). LowReds
speak mineSpeak (clipped, "o'" name prefix). Gold expletive:
"gorydamn." Red expletive: "bloodydamn" — outs a carved
Red, treat as major narrative event. Pan-Color: "slag it."
```

## 5. RACES — 14 COLORS {#races}

*Replaces all SRD races. Disable SRD races in platform settings.*

-----

### Gold (au)

**Description:** Apex of the Society’s engineered hierarchy. 6’2”–6’8”, golden irises, bone density bordering superhuman. Institute-forged into killers, patronage networks sharpen into politicians.
**Appearance:** Strong jaw, high cheekbones, patrician features. Lean muscle for combat. Peerless Scarred carry a diagonal scar across one cheek.
**Traits:** +2 STR, +2 CON, +1 mental stat. Speed 35 ft. Institute-Forged (proficiency: one martial weapon + Athletics/Intimidation/Persuasion). Peerless Bearing (Advantage Intimidation vs non-Golds). Gold Metabolism (resistance exhaustion, advantage CON vs poison). Languages: HighLingo, MidLingo.
**Portrait prompt:** `Tall physically perfect human, golden irises, sharp patrician features, Roman-Imperial bearing, sleek dark formal attire with subtle House insignia. Cold appraising gaze. Platinum and obsidian tones.`

-----

### Silver (ag)

**Description:** Financiers and industrialists. Brilliant, acquisitive, always calculating. Quicksilver is the archetype — large, bald, hunched simian shoulders, butcher’s hands. Not the slender stereotype.
**Appearance:** Large build, bald or silver-streaked hair. Calculating eyes. Understated wealth — perfect fabric, no logos.
**Traits:** +2 INT, +1 CHA. Speed 30 ft. Market Mind (proficiency Persuasion + Insight/Deception/History). Leverage (once per long rest, social Advantage via financial/informational hold). Languages: MidLingo, HighLingo (functional).

-----

### White

**Description:** Justices, ritualists, secular priesthood. Preside over the Bleeding Place, administer oaths, run BoQC proceedings. Not neutral — they serve the Society.
**Appearance:** Pale, white or silver hair regardless of age. Immaculate white robes. Cold serene eyes — process over outcome.
**Traits:** +2 WIS, +1 CHA. Speed 30 ft. Sacred Authority (advantage Persuasion/Intimidation invoking Society law; non-Gold NPCs WIS DC 14 or comply). Ritual Knowledge (proficiency History + Religion). Unimpeachable (advantage vs Charm/Deception). Languages: HighLingo, MidLingo.

-----

### Copper (cu)

**Description:** Administrators, lawyers, magistrates. Gold will translated into paperwork and quotas. Every mine has a Copper MineMagistrate. Systems are harder to fight than soldiers.
**Appearance:** Neat and precise. Copper-tinged or auburn hair. Sharp organized eyes.
**Traits:** +2 INT, +1 WIS. Speed 30 ft. Legal Architecture (proficiency History + Insight; advantage navigating bureaucratic systems). Paper Trail (once per long rest, produce documentation granting legal access). Administrative Access (proficiency forgery tools). Languages: MidLingo, HighLingo (functional).

-----

### Blue (xe)

**Description:** Navigator-pilots, the only Color trusted to fly torchShips. They see the entire solar system. They choose not to act.
**Appearance:** Lean, precise. Azure or steel-blue tinted hair. Sharp pale eyes, always calculating vectors.
**Traits:** +2 INT, +1 DEX. Speed 30 ft. Stellar Navigation (advantage piloting/navigation). Orbital Detachment (advantage vs fear/panic in crisis). Calculated Mind (advantage INT spatial reasoning/patterns). Languages: MidLingo.

-----

### Yellow

**Description:** Doctors and scientists. Heal you if you can pay. A Yellow who asks too many questions about why certain patients keep arriving is either an asset or a liability.
**Appearance:** Warm golden or amber hair. Practical builds. Perpetually tired eyes.
**Traits:** +2 INT, +1 WIS. Speed 30 ft. Medical Mastery (proficiency Medicine; stabilize dying as bonus action; advantage diagnose/treat/poison). Research Mind (advantage INT science/chemistry/biology). Off-the-Books (once per long rest, provide untraceable medical treatment). Languages: MidLingo.

-----

### Green (si)

**Description:** Coders and technicians. They write the HoloCan propaganda. Some of them know it’s propaganda.
**Appearance:** Lean, tech-worn. Green-tinged hair. Quick intelligent eyes. Always a device nearby.
**Traits:** +2 INT, +1 DEX. Speed 30 ft. Systems Access (proficiency technology tools; advantage hack/code/disrupt electronics). Ghost Protocol (once per long rest, erase digital evidence or plant false data). Signal Read (advantage intercept/decode communications). Languages: MidLingo.

-----

### Violet

**Description:** Artists and Carvers — surgeon-artists who reshape human flesh. A hunted faction works for the Sons, attempting to transform a lowRed into a Gold.
**Appearance:** Slender, aesthetic. Deep violet or indigo hair, often elaborate. Expressive hands — always moving.
**Traits:** +2 DEX, +1 INT. Speed 30 ft. Carver’s Touch (proficiency Medicine + surgical tools; perform body modification with time/equipment). Aesthetic Eye (advantage Perception for detail, forgery, disguise). Pain Architecture (once per long rest, ignore wound penalties 1 hour). Languages: MidLingo.

-----

### Orange

**Description:** Mechanics and engineers. Without Oranges the ships stop flying, the domes stop sealing, the air stops cycling. The Society has never once acknowledged this.
**Appearance:** Stocky, practical. Copper-orange or rust-brown hair. Permanently grease-stained hands.
**Traits:** +2 INT, +1 STR. Speed 30 ft. Maker’s Instinct (proficiency all engineering/mechanical tools; jury-rig with improvised materials). Systems Thinking (advantage INT to understand/disable/exploit mechanical systems). Structural Memory (advantage navigating complex mechanical environments). Languages: MidLingo.

-----

### Gray (ti)

**Description:** Soldiers, police, Lurchers, Praetorian Guards. Bred for discipline and loyalty. Most never question their orders. The ones who do end up in front of a firing squad — or recruited by Ares.
**Appearance:** Medium build, economical muscle, cropped hair, alert eyes. Always military posture. Segmented gray plating, T-visor helmet, rank insignia on left pauldron.
**Traits:** +2 CON, +1 STR or DEX. Speed 30 ft. Legionary Training (proficiency light/medium armor, shields, one martial weapon). Chain of Command (advantage vs fear; disadvantage Insight against Golds — conditioned deference). Garrison Instinct (proficiency Perception). Languages: MidLingo.

-----

### LowRed (o’)

**Description:** Subterranean helium-3 miners. Deliberately kept small and compact. Told Mars is not yet terraformed. It was. Three centuries ago.
**Appearance:** Under 5’5”, wiry, calloused hands, pale or ruddy skin, red-tinged hair. Clan tattoo on inner wrist. Thermal underlayer and tool harness, clan colors on collar.
**Traits:** +2 CON, +1 DEX. Size Small. Speed 25 ft. Helldiver’s Instinct (proficiency Athletics + Survival; advantage tunnel/confined/geothermal). MineSpeak (advantage Deception passing as non-threatening laborer). Compressed Frame (move through spaces one size smaller; advantage Stealth in low-light industrial). Conditioned Smallness (disadvantage Intimidation vs mid/highColors). Languages: MineSpeak, MidLingo (limited).
**Portrait prompt (male):** `Short wiry male, under 5'5", pale ruddy skin, red-tinged hair cropped close, heavy calloused hands, clan tattoo inner wrist. Worn thermal underlayer and tool harness. Low-ceilinged amber-lit mine tunnel. Weathered, exhausted, quietly defiant. --ar 2:3 --style raw`

-----

### HighRed (of)

**Description:** Surface-dwelling Reds — construction crews, atmospheric workers, torchShip deckhands. Know Mars was terraformed centuries ago. Most likely to understand the lie. Most likely to become Sons of Ares.
**Appearance:** 5’4”–5’9”, weathered tan or ruddy skin, practical sealed jumpsuits, mag-boots, goggles pushed up. Named “[given name] of [settlement].”
**Traits:** +1 CON, +1 DEX, +1 WIS. Speed 30 ft. Surface-Bred (proficiency Athletics + piloting/engineering/Stealth). The Knowing (advantage Insight — practiced at reading people who lie to them for a living). Midlingo Fluency (no social penalty passing as Brown or Orange). Languages: MidLingo, MineSpeak.

-----

### Brown

**Description:** Servants, cooks, nannies. Legally property in some jurisdictions. Invisible to highColors. They hear everything. The Sons have never invested enough in Brown recruitment. This is a mistake.
**Appearance:** Slight to medium builds, nondescript by design — engineered to be forgettable. Muted brown hair. Single given name only. Expression carefully neutral.
**Traits:** +1 DEX, +1 WIS, +1 CHA. Speed 30 ft. The Invisible (advantage Stealth in domestic/administrative spaces; highColor NPCs automatically fail to notice unless directly searching). Overheard (once per session, recall specific conversation from any location they’ve served). Necessary (never turned away from locations requiring domestic service). Languages: MidLingo.

-----

### Obsidian

**Description:** 7’4”–7’8” apex warriors engineered to be the most physically dangerous humans alive, then given a religion making them weapons in Gold hands. Almost impossible to recruit — their faith is structural, not incidental.
**Appearance:** Massively built, jet-black hair worn long or braided. Onyx-black eyes. Ritual scarring across face, arms, chest. Single name only — deep, harsh phonetics (Ragnar, Aja, Volga).
**Traits:** +3 STR, +2 CON, -1 INT. Size Large. Speed 35 ft. Apex Predator (advantage all STR checks/saves; +1d6 damage melee). Aesir’s Devotion (immunity fear; disadvantage attacks against Golds unless Gold ordered attack). Living Weapon (proficiency all weapons + unarmed; unarmed 1d8). Languages: MidLingo (halting).

-----

### Pink

**Description:** The pleasure caste, trained from childhood. They have access to every room in every palace. The Sons have criminally underused them.
**Appearance:** Physically striking by engineering — idealized features, graceful movement. Single given name, often musical (Evey, Harmony, Corel). Named without particle.
**Traits:** +2 CHA, +1 WIS. Speed 30 ft. The Performance (advantage Persuasion, Deception, Performance). Read the Room (advantage Insight; detect emotional states within 30 ft as free action). Every Door Open (never refused entry to domestic, social, or entertainment spaces). Languages: HighLingo, MidLingo.

### Aureate *(Gold — base class)*

The Gold ruling class, Institute-forged. What emerges depends on the Gold.
**Hit Dice:** d10 | **Primary:** STR/CON
**Core abilities:** Gold Physiology (advantage STR/CON; resistance exhaustion), Razor Born (proficiency razors sword/whip), Highborn Authority (non-Gold NPCs WIS DC 12 or defer instinctively), Institute Training (proficiency Athletics, Intimidation, one of History/Persuasion/Insight), The Passage (survive 0 HP once per long rest CON DC 10).
**Resource:** Dignitas

**Subclasses (choose at level 3):**

- **Iron Gold:** Conqueror’s Edge (+1d8 razor, crit 19-20), Iron Constitution (resist all physical damage one round per short rest), Living Legend (Gold NPCs defer tactically; non-Golds WIS DC 16 or fear-paralyzed). Resource: Glory.
- **Peerless Scarred:** Institute Memory (advantage INT tactical/political), Passage Survivor (0 HP survive CON DC 12), Patronage Network (once per session, call on Institute connections). Resource: Dignitas.

- **Pixie:** The Circuit (advantage all social checks in luxury environments), Dirt (recall compromising secret about Gold NPC once per session), Invisible Threat (advantage first combat attack — they never expect it). Resource: Indulgence.

### GANYMEDE

**Type:** Moon | **Faction:** House Codovan

**The Codovan Tier** | Faction: House Codovan

```
The alarm pulls you out of a shallow sleep at 0530.

Shift starts in ninety minutes. You know this before
you open your eyes — you've known it every morning
for eight months, the same way you know which bunk
creaks, which locker sticks, which stretch of corridor
between here and the depot checkpoint smells of
coolant leak.

Delta Sorin is already up. He's wrestling his work
harness over his shoulders with the particular
aggression of a man who hasn't had enough sleep
and knows the food printer is down again.

"Gorydamn processor's been out since third shift,"
he says, not looking at you. "Filed the maintenance
request myself. You know what Cael said? 'Log it
with Orange.' I logged it with Orange. You know
what Orange said?"

Through the window at the corridor's end, Jupiter
fills the dome glass — amber and cream and vast,
the way it always is, the way it was before you
arrived and will be long after. The depot loading
bay below it is already running. Gray checkpoint
lights blink steady at two-second intervals, 200
meters east.

From the common room, a Copper administrator's
voice carries through the HoloCan on its morning
loop: "...productivity targets for Cycle 847
reflect a 4% increase in helium-3 output
expectations, consistent with Society growth
projections and the ongoing noble work of—"

Someone turns the volume down.

Your locker is in front of you. Inside it: your
work harness, your tools, your forged sigil sitting
against your skin the way it always does — warm,
invisible, the weight of everything you are here
and everything you actually are underneath it.

Last night, Rotation 7's whisperCrystal drop
window opened.

You haven't checked the Melt yet.

What do you do?
```
