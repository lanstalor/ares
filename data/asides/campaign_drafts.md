# Red Rising TTRPG: Ganymede Campaign Concepts (728 PCE)

## Campaign 1: The Icarus Protocol
**Theme:** Heist, Betrayal, Syndicate Espionage

*   **Hook:** The players, a ragtag crew of LowColor smugglers and disillusioned Gray veterans, are hired by a shadowy Syndicate figure known only as "The Broker." Their mission: infiltrate the heavily guarded Ilium Shipyards on Ganymede and steal a shipment of experimental, hyper-refined Helium-3 destined for a new Gold dreadnought.
*   **Main Antagonist:** Praetor Valerius (Gray Garrison Commander). Valerius is a brutally efficient and highly decorated Gray who has recently been assigned to clean up the shipyards. He is secretly utilizing off-books interrogation techniques and has seeded the Syndicate with his own informants.
*   **Hidden-State Mechanic:** *The Heat Index*. The GM secretly tracks the players' visibility and the evidence they leave behind. As the Heat Index rises, Gray patrols increase, prices on the black market skyrocket, and Syndicate allies begin to sever ties or actively betray the party to save themselves.
*   **Act Structure:**
    *   *Act 1: The Setup:* Gathering intel, securing specialized gear from the Syndicate, and probing the shipyard's outer defenses. 
    *   *Act 2: The Score:* The heist itself, requiring synchronized sabotage, social engineering among the Brown and Pink staff, and bypassing elite Gray security.
    *   *Act 3: The Fallout:* Escaping the lockdown and attempting to deliver the payload while being hunted by Valerius's personal hit squad.
*   **Key Twist:** The Broker is not a Syndicate boss, but a rival Gold from House Bellona attempting to sabotage House Augustus's shipbuilding efforts. If the players succeed, they will be eliminated to tie up loose ends; the players must realize this and turn the tables, perhaps using the stolen Helium-3 as a bargaining chip or a very large bomb.

## Campaign 2: Echoes in the Rust
**Theme:** Medical Mystery, Biological Warfare, Grassroots Resistance

*   **Hook:** A horrific, aggressive respiratory disease dubbed "The Rust" is decimating the Red mining clans in Ganymede's deep crust. The players (miners, sympathetic Browns, or underworld fixers) discover that the disease is localized, unnatural, and ignores the standard inoculations. They must venture into abandoned, irradiated mining sectors to find the source.
*   **Main Antagonist:** Dr. Cassius au Arcos, a disgraced Gold geneticist using the Red population as unwitting test subjects for a bio-weapon designed to target specific Color genetic markers. He is protected by a fanatical, privately funded Gray mercenary company.
*   **Hidden-State Mechanic:** *Infection & Mutation Track*. Players exposed to the Rust or its vectors must make hidden checks. The GM tracks the progression. Early stages offer adrenaline-fueled stat boosts, masking the danger, before severe debuffs and potential death set in, forcing players to act recklessly to find a cure.
*   **Act Structure:**
    *   *Act 1: The Outbreak:* Surviving the initial quarantine, dealing with panic in the mines, and uncovering the first clues of unnatural origin.
    *   *Act 2: The Descent:* Navigating the dangerous, forgotten lower levels of Ganymede to locate Dr. Arcos's hidden facility, battling infected wildlife and automated defenses.
    *   *Act 3: The Cure:* Infiltrating the lab, confronting Arcos, and securing the data needed for an antidote before the local Gray Garrison sterilizes the entire sector to cover it up.
*   **Key Twist:** The local Syndicate faction, who initially helped the players, strikes a deal with Dr. Arcos. They will extract him and his research in exchange for weaponizing the disease for the Syndicate's use. The players must choose between saving their people and stopping a potential planetary bio-terror threat.

## Campaign 3: The Silk and Steel Conspiracy
**Theme:** High Society Intrigue, Corruption, Impending Coup

*   **Hook:** Operating in the glittering upper domes and the squalid mid-levels of Ganymede's capital, the players are high-end thieves and data-runners. They are hired to intercept a courier carrying a secure datapad belonging to the planetary governor. The datapad supposedly contains ledgers proving the governor is skimming Helium-3 profits.
*   **Main Antagonist:** Governor Octavia au Lune (or a loyalist proxy), a seemingly benevolent ruler who is actually deeply paranoid and ruthless. Her enforcer, a scarred Obsidian known only as "The Hound," hunts the players relentlessly through the city's underbelly.
*   **Hidden-State Mechanic:** *The Leverage Web*. Players accumulate dirt, favors, and blackmail material on various NPCs (Syndicate bosses, Gray officers, Copper administrators). The GM secretly maps how these NPCs are connected. Using leverage on one might unknowingly trigger retaliation from another, making every social interaction a minefield.
*   **Act Structure:**
    *   *Act 1: The Interception:* A high-speed chase and daring robbery to secure the datapad from the courier in the bustling mid-levels.
    *   *Act 2: The Decryption:* Working with paranoid hackers and Syndicate fences to crack the military-grade encryption while avoiding The Hound's increasingly violent sweeps.
    *   *Act 3: The Checkmate:* Deciding how to use the information, navigating the resulting power vacuum, and surviving the governor's wrath.
*   **Key Twist:** The datapad does not contain financial ledgers. It contains the master override codes for the capital's automated defense turrets and the deployment schedules for the entire Gray Garrison. The Syndicate faction that hired them plans to use these codes to disable the defenses and launch a full-scale, bloody uprising, intending to use the players as the fall guys for the initial breach.
