# LE EDITORIAL REVIEW: GANYMEDE CAMPAIGN CONCEPTS (728 PCE)

**TO:** Writing Team
**FROM:** Lead Editor
**SUBJECT:** Brutal Critique of Campaign Drafts - Do Better.

I just read through the campaign drafts for the 728 PCE Ganymede setting. Frankly, if you handed this to a true Red Rising fan, they’d feed you to the pitvipers. You’ve got some solid mechanical hooks and a good grasp of the tone, but your lore accuracy is a gorydamn mess. You are fundamentally misunderstanding the geopolitical landscape of the solar system in 728 PCE. 

Here is the brutal truth. Fix it or you're going to the deep mines.

---

## Campaign 1: The Icarus Protocol

### Lore Accuracy (FAIL)
You placed this in the Ilium Shipyards on Ganymede, which is a fantastic set-piece, but then you made the conflict between House Bellona and House Augustus. **Are you completely mad?** Ganymede and the Ilium Shipyards are the crown jewel of the **Rim Dominion**, ruled by **House Raa**. Core houses like Augustus and Bellona do not build their private dreadnoughts in the Rim without massive oversight from the Raa, and they certainly wouldn't be the primary political movers here. The twist completely ignores the existence of the Sovereign of the Rim and their fierce independence. 

### Tension & Agency (PASS)
The *Heat Index* is a solid, oppressive mechanic that fits the paranoid survival of LowColors operating under Gold's boot. The agency is good—giving them the Helium-3 as a bargaining chip or a WMD is exactly the kind of paradigm-shifting power LowColors need to survive in this universe.

### Grimdark Fit (PASS)
It feels like Pierce Brown. Ragtag crews, brutal Gray commanders, and double-crossing Golds. It has the right grime and syndicate espionage feel.

**Verdict:** Change the Core House conflict to a Rim-centric conflict. Have the twist involve a rogue Rim faction, or a Core spy trying to sabotage Rim shipbuilding capabilities, NOT a random Bellona/Augustus petty squabble.

---

## Campaign 2: Echoes in the Rust

### Lore Accuracy (CATASTROPHIC FAIL)
"Dr. Cassius au Arcos"? Did you just pull random Gold names from a hat? Cassius is a Bellona. Arcos is Lorn's house. Combining them sounds like a bad fanfiction OC. Furthermore, while Gold geneticists exist, Yellows and Carvers do the actual dirty lab work. A Gold might fund or oversee it, but they wouldn't be wearing a lab coat playing mad scientist in the mud. Also, testing a bio-weapon on your own Helium-3 mining workforce? That's economic suicide. Even the cruelest Golds know that no Reds = no Helium = no power. 

### Tension & Agency (PASS)
The *Infection & Mutation Track* is brilliant. Giving them a buff that masks a deadly debuff is incredibly grimdark and forces players to make desperate, bloody choices. The final twist—saving their people vs. stopping a bio-terror threat—gives the players agonizing agency. Perfect.

### Grimdark Fit (PASS)
Irradiated lower levels, dying Reds, corporate bio-warfare. It’s gritty, visceral, and horrifying. Very *Red Rising*.

**Verdict:** Fix the villain. Rename him, make him a sociopathic Carver or a rogue Yellow funded by an extremist Gold faction. Ensure the motive for targeting their own workforce makes sense—maybe it's a defunct, quarantined mine being used as an illegal black-site, not the primary Helium-3 veins.

---

## Campaign 3: The Silk and Steel Conspiracy

### Lore Accuracy (ABYSMAL)
"Governor Octavia au Lune"? I nearly choked on my whiskey. **Octavia au Lune is the Sovereign of the entire Society in 728 PCE.** She rules the solar system from the Citadel on Luna, surrounded by Olympic Knights. She is not a mid-tier planetary governor of a moon in the Rim. Putting her as a local boss fundamentally breaks the universe. If you want a paranoid governor, make up a prominent Raa cousin or a loyalist Rim Gold.

### Tension & Agency (MIXED)
The *Leverage Web* is a cool social mechanic, but it risks turning the game into a spreadsheet rather than a bloody fight for survival. Keep it narrative and fast-paced. The twist where they are being used as fall guys for a Syndicate uprising is appropriately bleak, but you need to ensure the players have the agency to discover this *before* the turrets turn on them. If they just get ambushed at the end, that's a railroad, not a game.

### Grimdark Fit (PASS)
The contrast between the glittering high society and the squalid mid-levels is core to the Society's aesthetic. An Obsidian enforcer named "The Hound" is a terrifying, classic antagonist that fits right in.

**Verdict:** Erase Octavia au Lune from this draft entirely. Replace her with a local Rim arch-governor. Give the players breadcrumbs to uncover the Syndicate's true plan early on so they can decide whether to stop the uprising, hijack it, or escape in the chaos.

---

**FINAL THOUGHTS:**
Mechanically and thematically, these drafts understand the brutality and tension of the *Red Rising* universe. But the lore is so wildly inaccurate it ruins the immersion. Learn the difference between the Core and the Rim, learn who rules what in 728 PCE, and stop using main characters where they don't belong. Make the edits and send them back to me by cycle's end.
