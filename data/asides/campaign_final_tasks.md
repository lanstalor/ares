# Campaign Final Plan: The Icarus Protocol (Revised)

## Concept Selection & Rationale
Based on the editorial critique, **Campaign 1: The Icarus Protocol** has been selected as the strongest foundation. While all drafts suffered from lore inaccuracies, Campaign 1 required the most straightforward adjustments (shifting from a Core House conflict to a Rim-centric conflict) while maintaining highly praised mechanics (The Heat Index) and a strong thematic fit for a gritty, heist-focused Red Rising module. 

## Revised Campaign Overview
**Theme:** Heist, Betrayal, Syndicate Espionage in the Rim
**Setting:** Ilium Shipyards, Ganymede (Rim Dominion, 728 PCE)
**Hook:** A crew of LowColor smugglers and disillusioned Gray veterans are hired by "The Broker" to steal experimental, hyper-refined Helium-3 from a new Rim dreadnought. 
**The Fix (Lore Accuracy):** The conflict is no longer a petty Augustus vs. Bellona squabble. The shipyards are rightfully overseen by **House Raa**. The Broker is revealed to be a deep-cover Core spy (perhaps a Gorgon operative of the Sovereign or a House Augustus agent) aiming to cripple Rim naval production to maintain Core supremacy.
**Main Antagonist:** Praetor Valerius, a brutal Gray Garrison Commander fiercely loyal to House Raa, tearing through the Syndicate to stop the theft.
**Hidden-State Mechanic:** *The Heat Index*. A tension clock tracking the party's visibility and escalating the Gray response.

## Action Plan
1.  **Lore Revision:** Completely rewrite the background lore document to center on House Raa's dominance over Ganymede and the looming threat of Core interference.
2.  **Mechanics Development:** Codify *The Heat Index* into a rigid mechanical system (e.g., a 10-segment clock) with specific triggers (failed stealth checks, leaving evidence, violent outbursts) and escalating consequences at each threshold.
3.  **NPC & Adversary Creation:** Stat out the key players, ensuring they reflect their Color and faction accurately. 
4.  **Worldbuilding & Cartography:** Design the operational zones of the Ilium Shipyards, from the squalid worker habs to the hyper-secure dreadnought drydocks.
5.  **Narrative Structure:** Flesh out the three-act structure, ensuring the twist (The Broker's true identity and plan to eliminate the crew) is foreshadowed but not obvious, giving players the agency to turn the tables.
